NDWI-Based Water Body Analysis in Ottawa Using Google Earth Engine

Overview: This project applies the Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) to analyze and visualize surface water bodies in Ottawa, Ontario, using cloud-based satellite imagery processing in Google Earth Engine (GEE). The project demonstrates how Earth observation data can be effectively used to support hydrological studies, urban planning, and environmental monitoring.

Objectives

  • Detect and delineate water bodies within the Ottawa region using Sentinel-2 imagery.
  • Enhance understanding of remote sensing indices and geospatial image processing.
  • Explore the capabilities of Google Earth Engine for real-time spatial analysis.

Methodology

  • Data Source: Sentinel-2 MSI Level-1C imagery, selected for high spatial resolution and multispectral coverage.
  • Index Used: NDWI = (Green - NIR) / (Green + NIR), designed to enhance the presence of water by contrasting reflectance in the green (Band 3) and near-infrared (Band 8) spectral bands.
  • Platform & Tools: Google Earth Engine (JavaScript API) for cloud-based processing, map visualization, and threshold-based classification of water features.
  • Preprocessing: Filtered by cloud cover and date range to ensure data quality and seasonal clarity (e.g., summer months for maximum water visibility).

Results

The analysis successfully highlighted various surface water features across the city, NDWI values were used to create a binary water mask, where water pixels were isolated based on a defined threshold (e.g., NDWI > 0.2). The resulting map provides a clear and accurate spatial distribution of water bodies within the study area.

Conclusion

This project showcases the potential of remote sensing and cloud-based geospatial platforms in environmental research. By leveraging NDWI and Sentinel-2 data, it was possible to effectively identify water bodies and support data-driven decision-making. The project also strengthened my skills in Earth Engine scripting, satellite image interpretation, and spatial analysis.
