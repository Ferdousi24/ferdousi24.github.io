Project Description

As part of my internship in the Department of Geography, Environment, and Geomatics at the University of Ottawa, I worked on a project utilizing the U-Net architecture for image segmentation. This deep learning model, designed specifically for semantic segmentation tasks, was used to differentiate squirrel images based on color, with a focus on black, brown, and other color variants.

Key Highlights:

  • Dataset Preparation: Collected, masked, and preprocessed a dataset of squirrel images, categorized by colors.
  • Model Implementation: Utilized the U-Net architecture to develop a model that accurately identifies and segments squirrels across various backgrounds.
  • Segmentation: Used the trained model .
